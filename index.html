About

Thanks for stopping by my page! I'm an incoming first-year at Georgia Tech, majoring in Computer Engineering and Materials Science, with varying interests in environmental science, biochemistry, and mathematics.

I grew up in Alpharetta, a suburb just north of Atlanta, Georgia and spent my early teens prototyping robots, practicing VFX/video production, playing tennis competitively, and enjoying the great outdoors.

I'm currently an incoming HS Intern at the CIPHER Lab under Georgia Tech's Research Institute, where I'll be developing a renewable aquatic floating testbed sensor for industrial monitoring applications.

At Georgia Tech, I currently conduct material and colloid research through the Renewable Bioproducts Institute in Dr. Paul Russo's Soft Matter Optics Lab, working on a project characterizing cellulose nanocrystals and latex in conjuction with the USDA Forest Products Lab.

Outside the lab, I serve as a student research advisor, robotics mentor, and youth outreach lead with organizations like Access Life America and Technology Without Borders, where I help bridge education gaps through hands-on STEM teaching and nonprofit leadership.
